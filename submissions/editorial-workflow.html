Editorial Workflow

Overview

Every manuscript submitted to JSM follows a standardized editorial workflow designed to ensure quality, fairness, efficiency, and research integrity.

Workflow Stages
STAGE 1
Initial Submission

The Editorial Office performs an administrative check to verify that:

all required files have been uploaded;
the manuscript complies with formatting requirements;
author information is complete;
declarations have been provided.
Incomplete submissions may be returned to the corresponding author before editorial assessment.
STAGE 2
Editorial Screening

The Editor-in-Chief or an Associate Editor evaluates whether the manuscript:

fits the Journal's scope;
demonstrates sufficient scholarly quality;
presents an original contribution;
satisfies ethical requirements.
Manuscripts that clearly fall outside the Journal's scope or fail to meet minimum quality standards may be rejected without external review.
STAGE 3
Similarity Check

All manuscripts are screened using plagiarism detection software before peer review.

If substantial overlap, duplicate publication, or unethical publication practices are identified, the manuscript may be rejected or returned for clarification.
STAGE 4
Double-Blind Peer Review

Suitable manuscripts are assigned to at least two independent reviewers with relevant expertise.

Neither authors nor reviewers know each other's identities throughout the review process.
STAGE 5
Editorial Decision

Based on reviewer reports, the handling editor recommends one of the following decisions:

Accept Minor Revision Major Revision Reject
The Editor-in-Chief makes the final editorial decision.
STAGE 6
Production

Accepted manuscripts proceed through the following production steps:

Copyediting Language editing Typesetting Proof correction DOI registration Online publication
STAGE 7
Publication

The final article is published online and subsequently assigned to an issue of the Journal.
