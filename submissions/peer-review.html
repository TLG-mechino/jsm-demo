Peer Review

Overview

JSM operates a double-blind peer review system to ensure independent, objective, and constructive evaluation of all submissions.

Each manuscript is reviewed by at least two qualified experts selected according to their academic expertise and publication record.

Review Model Double-blind
Reviewers Minimum Two
Objectives of Peer Review

The review process evaluates:

Originality Scientific quality Methodological rigor Policy relevance Theoretical contribution Clarity of presentation Ethical compliance
Reviewer Recommendations

Reviewers may recommend:

Accept
Minor Revision
Major Revision
Reject
Reviewer recommendations are advisory. The final publication decision rests solely with the Editor-in-Chief.
Confidentiality

All manuscripts are treated as confidential documents. Reviewers must:

Maintain confidentiality
Avoid conflicts of interest
Provide objective and constructive comments
Complete reviews within the requested timeframe
Author Revisions

When revisions are requested, authors should:

Respond to every reviewer comment
Submit a revised manuscript
Upload a detailed Response to Reviewers document
Highlight all modifications made
Failure to submit revisions within the specified period may result in withdrawal of the manuscript.
Publication Ethics

All reviewers, editors, and authors are expected to follow the Journal's Publication Ethics and COPE Principles throughout the review process.
