Peer Review Policy

Overview

The Journal of State Management (JSM) employs a rigorous double-blind peer review process to ensure the quality, originality, and scholarly integrity of all published research.

Under this model, the identities of both authors and reviewers remain anonymous throughout the review process. Manuscripts are evaluated solely on their academic merit, methodological rigor, originality, significance, and relevance to the Journal's aims and scope.

Academic merit Methodological rigor Originality Significance Relevance to scope
Reviewer Selection

Each manuscript that passes the initial editorial assessment is normally reviewed by at least two independent experts. Reviewers are selected based on their academic expertise, publication record, and absence of conflicts of interest.

Review Model Double-blind
Reviewers Minimum Two
The Peer Review Process Includes
Initial editorial screening
Similarity checking
Reviewer invitation
Independent peer review
Editorial evaluation
Revision (where required)
Final editorial decision
Editorial Decision

Editorial decisions are based on reviewer recommendations together with the Editor's independent assessment.

The final decision rests exclusively with the Editor-in-Chief.
