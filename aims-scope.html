Aims & Scope

Journal Overview

The Journal of State Management (JSM) is an international, peer-reviewed, open-access journal dedicated to advancing knowledge and scholarly debate in the fields of public administration, state management, governance, public policy, and related interdisciplinary areas. Published by the Academy of Public Administration and Governance, Ho Chi Minh National Academy of Politics, Vietnam, the journal provides a platform for rigorous academic research that contributes to both theoretical development and practical improvement in public sector governance.

JSM aims to foster a deeper understanding of how governments, public institutions, and governance systems operate and evolve in response to contemporary political, economic, technological, environmental, and social challenges. The journal encourages innovative research that informs policy design, administrative practice, institutional reform, and public sector performance at local, national, regional, and global levels.

The journal welcomes original research articles, review articles, policy analyses, and comparative studies employing quantitative, qualitative, mixed-methods, or interdisciplinary approaches. Particular attention is given to research addressing the experiences of emerging economies, developing countries, and transitional societies, while maintaining a global perspective and encouraging international scholarly exchange.

Topics covered by JSM include, but are not limited to:

Public administration and state management
Governance, public governance, and national governance
Public policy analysis, design, implementation, and evaluation
Administrative reform and public sector modernization
Government institutions and organizational performance
Digital government, e-governance, and public-sector innovation
Public service delivery and citizen-centered governance
Regulatory governance and administrative law
Transparency, accountability, integrity, and anti-corruption
Public finance, budgeting, and fiscal governance
Human resource management in the public sector
Decentralization, local governance, and intergovernmental relations
Sustainable development governance and environmental governance
Crisis management, resilience, and adaptive governance
Comparative public administration and international governance studies
Purpose & Contribution

JSM seeks contributions that combine methodological rigor with practical relevance and policy significance. The journal serves as a forum for scholars, researchers, policymakers, public administrators, and practitioners to exchange ideas, disseminate evidence-based findings, and promote innovative approaches to effective, accountable, and sustainable governance.

Through the publication of high-quality research, JSM contributes to the advancement of public administration scholarship and supports evidence-informed policymaking and state management practices worldwide.
