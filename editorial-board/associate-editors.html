Associate Editors

Role Overview

Associate Editors support the Editor-in-Chief by managing manuscripts within their areas of expertise. They coordinate the peer review process, recommend editorial decisions, and ensure that submissions are evaluated objectively, efficiently, and according to the Journal's editorial policies.

Each Associate Editor is selected based on academic achievements, editorial experience, and recognized expertise in one or more areas covered by the Journal.

Primary Responsibilities
Conduct preliminary editorial assessment
Select qualified peer reviewers
Monitor the review process
Evaluate reviewer reports
Recommend editorial decisions
Promote high-quality submissions
Support the continuous development of editorial policies
Editorial Areas

Associate Editors may oversee submissions in:

Public Administration Public Governance Public Policy Administrative Law Public Management Local Government Digital Government Sustainable Development Public Finance Comparative Governance Administrative Reform Public Leadership
