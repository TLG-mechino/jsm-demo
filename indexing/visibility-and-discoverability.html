Visibility and Discoverability

Overview

The Journal of State Management (JSM) is committed to maximizing the global visibility and discoverability of the research it publishes.

The Journal adopts internationally recognized publishing standards and digital technologies to ensure that articles can be efficiently identified, indexed, cited, and accessed by researchers worldwide.
Metadata Standards

Each published article includes comprehensive metadata, including:

Article title
Author names
ORCID iDs (where available)
Institutional affiliations
Abstract
Keywords
DOI
Funding information
Data Availability Statement
Publication dates
Reference list
Standardized metadata improves interoperability among publishers, indexing services, libraries, and scholarly search engines.
Persistent Identifiers

JSM employs persistent identifiers to improve long-term discoverability. These include:

Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs)
ORCID iDs for researchers
Crossref metadata registration
Persistent identifiers facilitate reliable citation, linking, and integration across international scholarly communication systems.
Search Engine Optimization

The Journal applies internationally accepted practices to improve article visibility in academic search engines. These include:

Structured metadata Search engine-friendly article pages Descriptive titles and abstracts Rich keyword indexing DOI-based linking Machine-readable metadata XML-based publishing standards where applicable
Global Discoverability

JSM continuously works to enhance article visibility through inclusion in scholarly databases, search engines, institutional repositories, and international indexing services.

The Journal's objective is to ensure that published research reaches scholars, policymakers, practitioners, and institutions around the world.
Supporting Research Impact

Improved discoverability increases:

Readership
Citations
Academic collaboration
Policy influence
International research visibility

JSM regards discoverability as an essential component of responsible scholarly publishing.
