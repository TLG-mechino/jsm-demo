Overview

The Journal of State Management (JSM) is committed to promoting excellence in scholarly peer review by supporting the continuous development of reviewers. High-quality peer review is fundamental to maintaining research integrity, improving manuscript quality, and advancing knowledge in public administration, governance, and public policy.

This Reviewer Training Centre provides practical guidance, educational resources, and professional development opportunities for both new and experienced reviewers. The materials are designed to strengthen reviewers' skills in evaluating manuscripts objectively, ethically, and constructively.

Whether you are reviewing for the first time or have extensive editorial experience, JSM encourages all reviewers to engage with these resources as part of their continuing professional development.

Why Peer Review Matters

Peer review is the foundation of scholarly communication. It serves several essential purposes:

Ensures the scientific quality of published research
Improves the clarity and rigor of manuscripts
Supports editors in making informed publication decisions
Identifies methodological weaknesses and ethical concerns
Promotes transparency, accountability, and research integrity
Strengthens confidence in published findings
By participating in peer review, reviewers contribute not only to individual manuscripts but also to the advancement of their academic disciplines.
The Principles of Good Peer Review

An effective peer review should be:

Independent
Reviews should be based solely on scholarly merit and free from personal, institutional, political, or financial bias.
Objective
Assessments should focus on evidence, methodology, originality, and academic contribution rather than personal opinions.
Constructive
Comments should help authors improve their work by providing specific, balanced, and actionable recommendations.
Confidential
All information contained in manuscripts must remain confidential before publication.
Timely
Reviewers should complete evaluations within the requested timeframe or notify the Editorial Office promptly if additional time is needed.
Before Accepting a Review Invitation

Before agreeing to review a manuscript, reviewers should consider the following questions:

Does the manuscript fall within my area of expertise?
Do I have sufficient time to complete the review?
Do I have any conflicts of interest?
Can I provide an objective and impartial assessment?
If the answer to any of these questions is negative, reviewers should decline the invitation promptly so that alternative reviewers can be identified.
How to Review a Manuscript

A high-quality review is typically conducted in several stages.

Step 1 — Read the Manuscript as a Whole

Begin by reading the manuscript without making detailed comments. Ask yourself:

What is the main research question?
Why is the study important?
Is the manuscript within the Journal's scope?
Does the paper make a meaningful contribution?
Step 2 — Evaluate the Scientific Content

Assess each section carefully.

Title
Is it concise and informative?
Does it accurately reflect the study?
Abstract
Does it summarize the study effectively?
Are the objectives, methods, findings, and conclusions clearly presented?
Introduction

Consider whether:

The research problem is clearly defined
The literature review is current and relevant
Research objectives are explicitly stated
Methodology

Review whether:

The research design is appropriate
Sampling methods are justified
Data collection procedures are adequately described
Analytical techniques are suitable
Ethical approval is reported where necessary
Results

Evaluate whether:

Findings are clearly presented
Tables and figures are appropriate
Statistical analyses are correctly interpreted
Results address the research questions
Discussion

Assess whether:

Conclusions are supported by evidence
Findings are compared with previous research
Theoretical and practical implications are discussed
Limitations are acknowledged
References

Check whether:

Important literature has been cited
References are current and accurate
Citation style is consistent
Evaluating Research Quality

When reviewing a manuscript, consider the following questions.

Originality
Does the manuscript present new knowledge or a novel perspective?
Significance
Will the findings contribute to theory, policy, or practice?
Methodological Rigor
Are the methods appropriate, transparent, and reproducible?
Validity
Are the conclusions supported by sufficient evidence?
Presentation
Is the manuscript well organized and clearly written?
Writing Constructive Comments

Constructive reviews should be:

Respectful Specific Evidence-based Solution-oriented
Rather than simply identifying problems, reviewers should explain why an issue matters and, where possible, suggest practical ways to improve the manuscript.
Structure of a Review Report

A well-organized review typically includes:

Summary
Provide a brief overview of the manuscript.
Major Comments
Focus on issues affecting scientific validity or originality.
Minor Comments
Identify grammatical errors, formatting inconsistencies, citation corrections, or minor clarifications.
Recommendation
Recommend one of the following:
Accept
Minor Revision
Major Revision
Reject
Common Mistakes Reviewers Should Avoid

Reviewers should avoid:

Making personal criticisms
Providing vague comments without explanation
Requesting unnecessary citations to their own work
Evaluating manuscripts outside their expertise
Revealing confidential information
Delaying reviews unnecessarily
Allowing personal opinions to influence recommendations
Ethical Issues in Peer Review

Reviewers should immediately inform the editor if they suspect:

Plagiarism Duplicate publication Fabricated or falsified data Manipulated images Inappropriate authorship Undisclosed conflicts of interest Unethical research involving human participants or animals
Reviewers should not conduct independent investigations or contact the authors directly.
Artificial Intelligence and Peer Review

JSM recognizes that artificial intelligence (AI) tools may assist reviewers with language editing or administrative tasks. However, reviewers remain fully responsible for the content, accuracy, confidentiality, and integrity of their evaluations.

Reviewers should not:

Upload confidential manuscripts to public AI systems
Use AI-generated recommendations without critical evaluation
Delegate scholarly judgment to AI tools
Any significant use of AI during the review process should be disclosed to the Editorial Office.

Reviewer Certification
JSM Reviewer Certificate
Upon completing designated training activities and demonstrating consistent, high-quality reviewing, reviewers may receive a certificate acknowledging their professional development and contribution to the Journal.

The certificate is intended to support academic recognition and may be included in professional portfolios or institutional service records.

Recommended Learning Resources

Reviewers are encouraged to explore additional resources on:

Research methodology Publication ethics Open science Data sharing Responsible AI Statistical reporting Academic writing Peer review best practices
The Journal also recommends consulting guidance published by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE), the International Committee of Medical Journal Editors (ICMJE), and other internationally recognized organizations dedicated to research integrity.
Continuous Improvement

JSM regards peer review as a scholarly skill that can be continually refined through experience, reflection, and ongoing learning. The Journal encourages reviewers to participate actively in training opportunities, share best practices with colleagues, and contribute to the continuous improvement of the peer review process.

By investing in reviewer development, JSM seeks to strengthen the quality, credibility, and international impact of research published in the Journal while fostering a global community of ethical, competent, and engaged reviewers.
