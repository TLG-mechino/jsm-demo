Become a Reviewer

Overview

JSM invites experienced researchers and practitioners to serve as peer reviewers and contribute to the advancement of international scholarship in public administration and governance.

Reviewers play a critical role in improving manuscript quality, supporting editorial decision-making, and promoting research integrity.

Reviewer Qualifications

Prospective reviewers should normally meet the following criteria:

Hold a doctoral degree or equivalent academic qualification
Demonstrate expertise in one or more areas covered by the Journal
Have a strong publication record in peer-reviewed journals
Be able to provide objective, timely, and constructive reviews
Have no conflicts of interest with manuscripts under review
Be committed to maintaining confidentiality throughout the review process
The Editorial Board may also invite experienced professionals and policymakers with recognized expertise to review manuscripts in specialized areas.
Areas of Expertise

JSM welcomes reviewers in fields including:

Public Administration Public Governance Public Policy Administrative Law Public Management Digital Government Public Finance Local Government Sustainable Development Leadership and Public Sector Innovation Comparative Governance Administrative Reform
How to Apply

Scholars interested in becoming reviewers are invited to submit the following information:

Curriculum Vitae (CV)
Institutional affiliation
Academic position
ORCID iD
Research interests
Publication list
Areas of reviewing expertise
Contact information
Applications will be evaluated by the Editorial Office and the Editor-in-Chief.
Benefits of Becoming a Reviewer

Reviewers receive opportunities to:

Contribute to international scholarly publishing
Stay informed about emerging research
Expand international academic networks
Develop editorial experience
Receive annual recognition from the Journal
Become eligible for future Editorial Board appointments
