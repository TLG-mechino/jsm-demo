Peer review process

CTUJoISD follows a rigorous double-blind peer-review process. The identities of the reviewers are not disclosed to the authors, and vice versa. All submitted manuscripts must undergo preliminary screening by editors, anonymous assessment by independent reviewers, revisions by authors, and a final acceptance decision by the Editor-in-Chief.

Manuscript submission

The corresponding author submits the manuscript via the online system at ctujs.ctu.edu.vn. Submission through email is not encouraged.

Preliminary screening and plagiarism check

Editors check whether the manuscript fits the scope and adheres to journal guidelines. All submissions are screened for textual similarity using Crossref Similarity Check (iThenticate). If it passes, a section editor is assigned to handle the review process.

Recommendation of peer-reviewers

The section editor appoints two peer-reviewers of the relevant field. The review concludes with one of the following recommendations: acceptance, minor revisions, major revisions, resubmission for review, resubmission elsewhere, or rejection.

Review assessment

After considering the reviewers' assessment, the section editor will decide: manuscript acceptance, revisions required, or rejection.

Revisions

The author is required to revise the manuscript in response to reviewer and section editor comments, accompanied by a point-by-point response. All changes should be highlighted.

Final decision

After considering all reports and revisions, the editorial board makes a final decision. When all editorial issues are resolved, the manuscript is formally accepted, then edited, typeset, and published.

2
Special issues guidelines

The Journal invites scholars to guest edit special issues. Guest editors' credentials are checked and approved by the Editorial Board and the Editor-in-Chief.

Special issue proposal requirements

The topic must be within the scope of the Journal. The title should clearly reflect the subject of interest, accompanied by a summary and relevant keywords. Proposals must include a submission deadline, information on guest editors (name, affiliations, email, research interests), and details on at least 15 planned papers.

Guest editor responsibilities and ethics

Guest editors must ensure published content aligns with the journal's scope. Their own submissions must constitute no more than 25% of the issue's total. All submissions must undergo double-anonymous peer review, and confidentiality of all submitted material must be protected.

Editorial review process

All submissions follow the same peer review process as regular papers. After undergoing double-anonymous peer review, accepted submissions are published and labelled as belonging to a special issue.

Authors retain copyright on their articles but grant CTUJoISD — identified as the original publisher — the rights of first publication of the work.

4
Open access policy

CTUJoISD provides immediate open access to its published articles to the public, to broaden opportunities for high-quality research findings to be widely disseminated free of charge, contributing to the greater exchange of knowledge.

CTUJoISD permits everyone to read, download, copy, distribute, print, search, or link to the full texts of published articles without registration, price barriers, or permission from the Journal or the author.

8
Regulations on the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and editorial support tools
Mandatory disclosure

Authors are required to disclose any use of AI tools or editorial support software (e.g., ChatGPT, Grammarly, QuillBot, MidJourney), including the tool name, version, provider, purpose, and scope of use, clearly stated in the Acknowledgments or Methods section.

Author responsibility

AI tools cannot be listed as authors. Human authors bear full responsibility for the scientific content, accuracy, originality, and transparency of the manuscript. All AI-assisted content must be verified, edited, and remain under human accountability.

Limitations of use

Authors must not rely on AI to generate scientific ideas, data analyses, research conclusions, illustrations, or coding without independent human verification. Uploading sensitive or unpublished research data to public AI platforms is prohibited.

Peer review and editorial process

Editors, reviewers, and editorial collaborators are not allowed to use AI tools to draft review reports, evaluate unpublished manuscripts, or make editorial decisions, to ensure confidentiality, transparency, and objectivity.

Breach of ethics

Manuscripts that fail to disclose AI use, or misrepresent such use, will be considered a violation of publication ethics and may be rejected, retracted, or subjected to appropriate sanctions.

Policy updates

This regulation will be periodically reviewed and updated to remain consistent with advances in AI technology and international standards of research publishing ethics.

9
Data repository policy

CTUJoISD requires authors to provide a Data Availability Statement in all submissions. Authors are encouraged to deposit datasets in recognized public repositories (e.g., Zenodo, Figshare, Dryad, institutional repositories) and cite them with a DOI or permanent link.

If data cannot be shared, authors must explain the reasons (ethical, legal, or confidentiality restrictions). Authors are responsible for selecting appropriate repositories that ensure long-term preservation and accessibility.

10
Advertising policy

CTUJoISD is committed to maintaining the integrity and independence of its scholarly content. The journal does not accept advertising in any form. All editorial decisions are made independently of commercial influence, and published content is based solely on academic merit and peer review.
