Special Issue: Advancements in Civil Engineering — Integrating AI, Digital Twins, and Industry 4.0 Innovations
2025-08-30
Special Issue

The civil engineering and construction sectors are undergoing a profound transformation driven by the convergence of cyber-physical systems and Industry 4.0 technologies. The integration of AI, Digital Twins, Additive Manufacturing, Nanotechnology, and IoT is moving from theoretical research to large-scale practical application.

This Special Issue aims to curate high-quality original research articles and review papers documenting recent developments in AI and Industry 4.0 applications within civil engineering.

Key goals of this issue:

Accelerating project timelines from design to completion.
Enhancing structural safety, resilience, and sustainability.
Optimizing resource management, logistics, and operational efficiency.
Creating new pathways for smart materials, structures, and autonomous systems.
Keywords
Machine Learning & Deep Learning Computer Vision (CV) Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) Natural Language Processing (NLP) Generative AI Digital Twins AI-augmented BIM Robotics and Automation Optimization Algorithms Additive Manufacturing AI-driven Materials Science IoT and Sensor Networks Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS)

Special Issue: Modern Mechanics and Emerging Applications for Industry 4.0
2025-06-30
Special Issue

The convergence of mechanical sciences with digital technologies, smart materials, and intelligent systems is reshaping modern engineering within Industry 4.0. Traditional paradigms in structural analysis, material modeling, and mechanical system design are being augmented by computational mechanics, additive manufacturing, AI, and cyber-physical systems.

This Special Issue welcomes selected extended papers from ICOMMA2025 (Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam, October 17–19, 2025) and is strictly open to external submissions for broad participation and global impact.

Keywords
Modern mechanics Computational mechanics Mechanics 4.0 Multiphysics modeling Smart materials and structures Additive manufacturing Data-driven modeling Machine learning in mechanics Bioinspired structures Cyber-physical systems AI-driven design

Special Issue: Big Data, AI, and Machine Learning in Transportation
2021-11-16
Special Issue

Big Data is predicted to profoundly impact the transportation and logistics sector, accounting for around 15% of global GDP. AI and machine learning (AIML) may assist in solving the big data problem by routinely outperforming humans in specialized cognitive tasks.

This Special Issue proposes assessing the progress and synergy of tackling these two connected challenges. High-quality original research papers on "Big Data, AI, and Machine Learning in Transportation" are welcome.
